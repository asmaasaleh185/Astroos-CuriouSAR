Brief History about SAR. . .

Each slide is a chapter — click arrows or let it play. Images float as if seen from orbit.
1960s — The Spark
Early airborne radar experiments proved that coherent motion processing could create high-resolution images. These aircraft tests became the seed for Synthetic Aperture Radar — the idea that movement creates detail.
1978 — Seasat
Seasat was NASA's first ocean-observing SAR satellite. Though short-lived, it demonstrated radar's ability to map waves, sea ice, and coastal features from space.
1990s — Shuttle & Science
Space Shuttle missions and improved processing revealed forests, hidden channels, and subtle surface deformation — SAR became a scientific workhorse for geology and ecology.
Today — Sentinel & NISAR
Sentinel-1 and upcoming missions like NISAR provide continuous, global radar coverage — essential for monitoring climate, disasters, and Earth's changing face.
